# QUESTION

Given that:
$$\text{arg}\left(\frac{d+6i}{1-di}\right)=\frac{\pi}{4}$$ 
find the possible value(s) of $d$, where $d$ is a real constant.

# SOLVING

First, I decided to ratioanlize the complex number since I can see an imaginary number in the denominator.

> TIP: If you see a root/imaginary number in the denominator, always try to rationalize first.

$$\text{arg}\left( \frac{d+6i}{1-di} \times \frac{1+di}{1+di}\right)=\frac{\pi}{4}$$

$$\text{arg}\left( \frac{(d+6i)(1+di_{})}{1+d^{2}} \right)=\frac{\pi}{4}$$

$$\text{arg}\left( \frac{d+d^{2}i+6i+6di^{2}}{d^{2}+1} \right)=\frac{\pi}{4}$$

$$\text{arg}\left( \frac{d^{2}i+6i-5d}{d^{2}+1} \right)=\frac{\pi}{4}$$

$$\text{arg}\left( \frac{-5d+i(d^{2}+6)}{d^{2}+1} \right)=\frac{\pi}{4}$$

Then, I decided to change it to a form of $a+bi$:

$$\text{arg}\left( \frac{-5d}{d^{2}+1} + \frac{d^{2}+6}{d^{2}+1}i \right)=\frac{\pi}{4}$$

I got stuck here. I have spent over 2 weeks trying to solve this question but kept failing.\
A few weeks later from that stage, I made no progress until I was at church on Sunday, and suddenly came to my mind.

*"If the argument is $\frac{\pi}{4}$, which is 45 degrees, wouldn't $a$ and $b$ have to be equal the same???"*

I was in the middle of a church service so I couldn't do anything, however as soon as service finished, I rushed to grab a pen and paper. Fortunately, my thought was correct. Making $a=b$ would return a quadratic function which returns two negative numbers.

$$\frac{-5d}{d^{2}+1}=\frac{d^{2}+6}{d^{2}+1}$$

$$-5d=d^{2}+6$$

$$d^{2}+5d+6=0$$

$$d=-2, or -3$$

Therefore, doing all these would let me get a score of **E8**.
