#L3CALC 

---

'Modulus' (plural 'moduli') is the distance of the point $z \space(x+iy)$ from the origin of the argand diagram.

Modulus is written as $|z|$. Absolute value is added, since the negative/positive signs don't really matter, since it's *just* the distance from the origin.

To calculate distance, we can use the distance formula from L2 CoordGeo:

$$DISTANCE=\sqrt{ (x_{2}-x_{1})^{2}+(y_{2}-y_{1})^{2} }$$

But since we don't have $x_{2}$, we just use the following Pythagorean theorem:

$$|z|=\sqrt{ x^{2}+y^{2} }$$

For instance, for the complex number $z=2+4i$, the modulus $|z|$ can be calculated by the following:

$$|z|=\sqrt{ 2^{2}+4^{2} } \approx 4.472135955$$

Again, this represents the distance of the line (from origin to complex number) from the origin.

---

We also know that:

$$|z|=|\overline{z}|$$

Why? Because that the modulus is *just* the distance from the origin. As mentioned in *Note 4*, the conjugate of $z$ ($\overline{z}$), is just mirrored by the real axis. 

Since it is just mirrored by the real axis, it will have the same modulus.
