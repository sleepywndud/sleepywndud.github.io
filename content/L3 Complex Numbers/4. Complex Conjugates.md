In Complex Numbers, 'conjugate' is a term that's widely used. Since it is already explained in *Note 1*, not many will be discussed.

The conjugate of $z$ is written as $\overline{z}$. With a bar on the top (I call it overline).
For instance, if $z=x+iy$, $\overline{z}=x-iy$.

In the Argand plane, conjugate of a complex number corresponds to the reflection of the point in the real axis.
