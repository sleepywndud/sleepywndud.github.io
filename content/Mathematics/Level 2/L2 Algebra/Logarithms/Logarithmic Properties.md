# <u>Using Log Properties</u>:
### Common Logs:
Before starting, it is essential to know that when the base of $\log$ is empty, the base is 10.

$$
\log x=\log_{10}x
$$

This is just like how:

$$
\sqrt[2]{ x }=\sqrt{ x }
$$

### Logging Numbers:
$\log$ging a number also works. You can log ANY positive numbers:

$$
\log 2
$$

$$
\log 5
$$

However, when you $\log$ a number, you must log the other side of the equation: $$2=2$$

$$
\log 2=\log 2
$$

### Exponents:
When you log a number like $2^{2}$:

$$
\log 2^{2}
$$

The exponent can come down as a product:

$$
2\times (\log 2)
$$

In the calculator, $\log 2^{2}$ and $2\times(\log 2)$ will return the same result. 

### Log Rules:

$$
\log a+\log b=\log ab
$$

$$
\log a-\log b=\log \frac{a}{b}
$$

$$
\log_{a}b=\frac{\log b}{\log a}
$$
