For 'Excellence', an 'inequality' using discriminants must be found (+ linking the context).

For example, when you have the following two functions:
$$
y=\frac{1}{x-2}+3
$$
$$y=-kx+4$$
and you're asked to find the suitable range of values for $k$, so that the linear line **doesn't touch** the hyperbola. This is done using discriminants. 

> [!tip] Referring back to Discriminants
> In Algebra, we used discriminants to find how many 'solutions', or 'intersections' along the $x$ axis there are for a function. For an equation like: 
> $$
> ax^{2}+bx+c=0
> $$
> we can also say that the equation is equal to $y$:
> $$
> y=ax^{2}+bx+c=0
> $$
> Solving (factorizing) that quadratic function returns the values for $x$, where $y=0$. 
> The discriminant of that quadratic function finds how many times the function intersects where $y=0$ (which is the $x$-axis).

Now, going back to Systems of Equations, when we try to find the intersections, we make the two functions equal the same:
$$
\frac{1}{x-2}+3=-kx+4
$$
Then we remove the denominator:
$$
1+3(x-2)=(-kx+4)(x-2)
$$
$$
1+3x-6=-kx^{2}+2kx+4x-8
$$
Reorder:
$$
-kx^{2}+2kx+x-3=0
$$
Grouping like terms:
$$
-kx^{2}+(2k+1)x-3=0
$$

Now, the discriminant *of the combined equation* indicates how many times the functions **intersect each other** (hyperbola and linear).

$$
\Delta=(2k+1)^{2}-4(-k)(-3)
$$
Now, we must give the discriminant a value in order to calculate $k$. 

Since the question is asking us to find the values for $k$ so that there are no intersections among the two functions, the discriminant must be below zero:
$$
(2k+1)^{2}-4(-k)(-3)<0
$$
$$
4k^{2}+4k+1-12k<0
$$
$$
4k^{2}-8k+1<0
$$
$$
k \approx 0.134 \text{ or } 1.866
$$
$k$ would be between the values:
$$
0.134<k<1.866
$$
for there to be no intersections.
