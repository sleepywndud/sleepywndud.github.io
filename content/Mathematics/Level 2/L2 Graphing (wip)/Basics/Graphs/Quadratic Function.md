There are two types of parabola functions that you can draw:
- The $x$ **vertex form**,
- and the **intercept form**

These are the equations respectively:

$$y=a(x-p)^2+q$$

$$y=a(x-\alpha)(x-\beta)$$

where $(p,q)$ is the vertex coordinate of the parabola.

$a$ is the scale factor of the parabola, transforming it a narrow/wide function.

The $\alpha$, and $\beta$ are the intercepts along the $x$-axis.

You can translate the parabola by modifying the $p$, and the $q$ value. 

However, when translating, you must invert the signs (try it yourself online!)
