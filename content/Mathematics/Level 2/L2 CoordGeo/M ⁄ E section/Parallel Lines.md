Parallel lines are a pair of lines that never touch each other. This means that their gradient must be equal in order to not intersect each other.

Examples of parallel lines:

$$y=2x$$

$$y=2x+22$$

These two lines above are parallel as they have the same gradient.

To prove this, use the equation below: (like, write the exact equation down to prove)

$$m_{1}=m_{2} \space\space\space\text{   in order to me parallel}$$

I have no idea why, but in the exam, you are *required* to show that the two lines are parallel to each other. That's why I have put the equation above.

Other schools/classes might have a different way of proving that. Since this is an internal exam, **follow your school's way of proving so.**

(my school used that equation written above)
