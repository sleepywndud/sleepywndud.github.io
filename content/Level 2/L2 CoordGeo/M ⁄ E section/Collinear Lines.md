Collinear is when there is more than three points on plotted, and if they go on a same line.
<img src="/images/d8c5e68824d1b94cd6f1050ea72588ff.png" style='width: 300px;' /><img src="/images/8ba77aa33a8df6e82135e864cffaea56.png" style='width: 300px;' />
The above four blue points are collinear, as they go on the same line.

To determine whether a series of points are collinear or not, you have to figure out the gradient by using the gradient formula: ([[Gradients (m)]])
$$=(\frac{x_{1}+x_{2}}{2}, \frac{y_{1}+y_{2}}{2})$$
It is sufficient to prove that the dots are collinear just by using two (do three just in case, though).
