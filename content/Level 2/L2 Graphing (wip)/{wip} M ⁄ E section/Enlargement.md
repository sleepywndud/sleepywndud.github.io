## <u>Enlargement:</u>
To enlarge a function, you have to stretch the graph both horizontally and vertically:
*This has a very rare chance of appearing in the exam.*

##### <u>Vertical:</u>
To stretch this function vertically, we have to multiply everything in the equation by the number that scales the new graph.
To do this, I can replace $y \to \frac{y}{q}$, since rearranging the equation (to make $y$ the subject) would make everything multiplied by $q$.

Therefore, for the vertical stretch, I would have to multiply everything by $q$, by replacing $y$ as $\frac{y}{q}$.
$$y\to \frac{y}{q}$$
##### <u>Horizontal:</u>
To stretch this function horizontally, we have to make the scale factor of the function lower. This makes it so that the graph is flattened.
To do this, I can replace $x\to \frac{x}{p}$, since this would be the same as changing the scale factor of the function $x$.
$$x\to \frac{x}{p}$$
<center>same as:</center>
$$x\to \frac{1}{p}(x)$$
