## <u>Generic Equation:</u>
$$y=A\sin (B(x-p))+q$$
Where $A$, is the **amplitude**, which determines the height (crest) of the graph, marked by a red arrow on the image below:
<img src="../../../img/a74768e157fdff1ff26262e5922cebe9.png" style='width: 300px;' />
$y=(1)\sin (x)$
If the amplitude is increased to $2$, the size of the 'crest' will change.

while $B$ is the **frequency**, where frequency determines how many the graph concaves up/down within the period. If the frequency $(B)$ is set to a high number, the graph would have more local maximum/minimums. 

Moreover, $p$, and $q$ can be modified to translate the graph along the $x$ and $y$ axis.

## <u>Period:</u>
Period is a range inside a $\sin \text{/} \cos$ graph marked by the red range below:
<img src="../../../img/dbb8df4d41d4c2e3c78c831229773bf4.png" style='width: 300px;' />
$y=(1)\sin(x)$
It is the range between the first inflection point to the next next inflection point after going through the local maximum and minimum once.

We can calcualte the period when we have the frequency using the formula:
$$P=\frac{2\pi}{B}$$
..where $B$ is the frequency of the sine graph. 
In this case $(y=\sin x)$, the period would be $2\pi$, since the frequency $B$ is $1$.
