A logarithmic equation is basically the inverted function of a [[Exponential Function]] function; logarithmics are related to exponentials.

Equation of the function below: $y=\log_{2}x$
<img src="/images/3f2313c2ffc18a9876764a9bf5cbdd1e.png" style='width: 300px;' />
the generic equation is:
$$y=\log_{a}(x-p)+q$$
..where you can translate the graph by modifying the $p$ and $q$ coordinates. 
By modifying the value of $p$, you can translate the graph along the $x$ axis,
..and by modifying the value of $q$, you can translate the graph along the $y$ axis.

$a$ again is the scale factor of the function, where if you increase it, the function turns into a more sleep slope.
However, $a$ cannot be lower or equal to 1, as it would make the graph linear, or undefined. 

The asymptote of this function is the value of $p$. 
(Asymptotes are explained in the [[Exponential Function]] page.)

If you have noticed from the two graphs from the exponential, and the logarithmic,
<img src="/images/2b0fc7f391e94c46c5b2ff4a93f0c4d2.png" style='width: 300px;' />
..these two graphs mirror each other. Equations are below:
$$y=2^x$$
$$y=\log_{2}x$$

As mentioned twice, exponentials are related to logarithms, and using a table, you can see that the $x$ and $y$ pairs are inverted.
