Logarithms are a way of determining "how many times you have to multiply a number, to form another number".

For instance, if you have the number 8, you can represent that number in terms of 2 like this:

$$2\times 2\times 2=8$$

Or, you can represent it as:

$$2^3=8$$

But, what happens when we have a number like 4096?

It will be something like this:

$$2\times 2\times 2\times2\dots$$

And will be able to be represented as:

$$2^x=4096$$

Logarithms are perfect in cases where the exponent is unknown. 

Before we use logarithms, we have to look at the properties of numbers.

In the above equation ($2^x=4096$), we know that 2 is the base, $x$ is the exponent, and 4096 is the product. 

In logarithms, there are properties, too just like this:

$$\log_{\boxed{BASE}}\boxed{ARGUMENT}=\boxed{EXPONENT}$$

We put the base as 2, and argument as 4096. This tells us "how many times we have to multiply 2 by itself to form 4096":

$$\log_{2}4096=x$$

$\log_{2}4096$ can be put into a graphic calculator straight to get the answer. In scientific calculators, we have to use one of the log properties (or rules). 

$$\therefore x=12$$
